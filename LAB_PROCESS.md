# Lab Process: Developing an Automated Compliance Strategy

This document tracks the strategic decision-making process for the second laboratory, focusing on transitioning from general security concepts to actionable IT Operations tooling.

## 1. Skill Selection & Justification
**The Problem:** Moving beyond basic cybersecurity theory to demonstrate hands-on auditing capabilities.
**Process:** Evaluated three specialized paths: 
1. Log Analysis.
2. Compliance Auditing (Selected).
3. Network Vulnerability Scanning.

**Decision Logic:** As an aspiring **IT Operations Analyst**, the focus was placed on **Compliance Auditing** because it aligns with infrastructure scalability and standard operating procedures (SOPs) used in enterprise environments.

## 2. Technical Constraints & Environment
*   **Platform**: Utilizing local hardware (Beelink SER5 MAX) to simulate a production server environment.
*   **Automation Language**: Python was chosen due to its versatility in handling system-level tasks and generating structured data.
*   **Auditing Standard**: CIS (Center for Internet Security) Benchmarks were selected as the source of truth to ensure the audit follows global industry standards.

## 3. Collaborative Iteration
**Prompting for Personalization:** Instead of following a generic guide, the project was tailored through a recursive Q&A session to ensure the technical output (the Python script) and the professional output (the Audit Report) met the specific requirements of a high-level cybersecurity role.

**Key Outcomes:**
*   Prioritized **Detection and Analysis** over passive defense.
*   Focused on generating a **Remediation Plan** as part of the final deliverable.
*   Established a 3-step action plan for the week: Benchmark Research, Python Scripting, and Reporting.
